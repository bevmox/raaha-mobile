## 1. The problem I focused on, and why

The brief's own sentence is the problem: *"Employee wellbeing is a growing priority across Abu Dhabi government, but nothing that exists today feels personal, or fits into someone's actual day."* That sentence contains two distinct failures.

**Failure 1 — not personal.** The wellbeing infrastructure Abu Dhabi government provides today is excellent at a population level, and identical at an individual level. ADPHC's Workplace Wellness Program reaches 38 entities with webinars, Health Kiosks, and screenings. The National Programme for Happiness & Wellbeing trained 60 Chief Happiness and Wellbeing Officers — roughly one per 500 employees at DGE's scale. The Academy of Wellbeing and Friends of Happiness platform are federal resources that don't know whose Tuesday it is. Every instrument is institutional care provided *to* the employee — well-designed, policy-grounded, and generic by architecture.

**Failure 2 — doesn't fit the day.** Everything that exists assumes employees have surplus time to *do wellbeing* — attend a webinar, book a screening, complete a reflection, build a habit. For a Grade 10 section head with six meetings before lunch, surplus time is the one thing the day does not contain. The tools go unused. Not because employees don't care — because the tools were built for a day that isn't theirs.

**The evidence the corpus surfaces** — 40% daily work stress (Gallup), 55%+ burnout symptoms among UAE professionals (KPMG 2023), 16% with manager–subordinate tension (WPO), 3 in 5 can't switch off outside work hours, Gulf "push-through" culture that rewards overwork and stigmatises fatigue (Ellusho) — converges on one population: **middle management, carrying their own deliverables plus the welfare of their teams.** That's who I designed for.

**Why this problem, not a different one.** Abu Dhabi government has already solved the other problems. Population wellbeing — ADPHC. Policy — HR Law 08/2025. Leadership — CHO program. Strategy — National Wellbeing 2031. What's unsolved is **the last mile**: the gap between the provision and the moment it's needed. That's the gap AI is uniquely able to close.

## 2. Who I designed for

**Hamad Al Mazrouei. Grade 10. Section Head, Department of Government Enablement. Team of eleven. Six years in.**

Hamad is the archetype the evidence corpus converges on. He owns his own deliverables — policy notes, entity coordination, the slide his director will present on Thursday — and he owns the welfare of eleven people, which no one above him has ever formally asked him to own. The second job is the one he carries home.

**Why a G10 section head, specifically:**

- **Scale.** Middle management is the largest layer of Abu Dhabi government by headcount and the most under-served by current wellbeing provision. CHOs work with senior leadership; ADPHC programs skew toward frontline employee health. Section heads are the gap.
- **Plausibility.** HR Law 08/2025's flexible-work provisions and the Al Jadaara Leadership Development Program are both designed for this grade band. DGE has recognised this layer needs support — the provision exists, the surfacing doesn't.
- **Evidence density.** Every major stressor in the research corpus — manager tension, burnout, "push through" culture, inability to switch off, work-life imbalance — lands hardest on section heads.
- **Insider credibility.** I spent a year as Head of Digital, Visitor Experience at DCT Abu Dhabi and worked at mandate level for the Effortless CX Strategy at Sia Partners. I know how a G10 writes messages, what his Tuesday actually contains. The persona isn't researched — it's recognised.

**Design constraints Hamad imposes on the product:**

- His register is warm, brief, ceremonial when needed, operational by default. No wellness-industry vocabulary — no "check-in," no "self-care," no "mindfulness moment."
- His time windows are narrow — ten minutes between meetings, twenty minutes at the end of the day. Anything that needs more doesn't fit.
- His privacy is non-negotiable. More than half of UAE employees don't feel free to discuss mental health with managers. Nothing Raaha does can leave a trail his director sees.
- His artefacts have to be real. A section head does not have patience for journaling. Every interaction ends with something that changed in the world — a meeting moved, a message sent, a location chosen, a loop closed.

**Deck-level range:** The process deck narrates three archetypes (Hamad, plus a frontliner at shift-opening and an HR-Law surfacing moment around caregiving). The prototype goes one deep on Hamad. One engine, many surfacing moments — the depth cut proves the platform.

## 3. The hypothesis — what Raaha actually does

**In one sentence:** *Raaha produces rest — the specific Arabic meaning of راحة: ease, peace of mind, tranquility — inside the working day, by clearing operational weight from the employee's path and then offering restoration that's shaped like their life.*

**Why this is the right definition of wellbeing for a civil servant.** Rest, for Hamad, is not a meditation session. Rest is not carrying Reem home at dinner. Rest is closing the laptop at 17:20 without reopening it at 22:30. Rest is arriving at his daughter's bedtime present, not spiked. Rest is sleeping without the 1:1 replaying. Rest is accumulating, over months, the capacity to keep leading eleven people — rather than depleting toward burnout at month 9.

Every one of those outcomes is wellbeing. None of them is delivered by a meditation library. All of them are downstream of two things happening in sequence: **the operational weight that prevents rest is removed, and something restorative takes its place.**

That's Raaha's two-layer architecture.

## 4. The two layers

### Layer 1 — Orchestration (creates the space)

Raaha reads Hamad's day in real time — calendar, comms, timestamps, unfinished loops. When it identifies a moment where operational weight is blocking the employee's rest, it proposes specific actions and produces the artefacts to execute them. Meetings moved. Messages drafted. Loops closed.

This layer is productivity-shaped. That's deliberate. Operational friction is where the damage to rest accumulates for a civil servant — so removing it is how rest is produced. The mechanism is operational; the purpose is somatic.

### Layer 2 — Restoration (inhabits the space)

Once Raaha has cleared the operational load, it asks what to do with the space. Not as a breathing exercise by default. As a swipe-based, contextually-generated check-in that reads what the employee lived through today and offers something small, specific, and restorative.

Two-second inputs. One-sentence responses. A single opt-in act — a change of location, a reshape of an existing meeting, a walking 1:1 instead of a seated one, a protected block before a heavy conversation. Chosen, not imposed.

**This is what "take better care" means in a civil servant's day.** Not a wellness feature. A moment of grounding, tuned to what Raaha knows about him, inside the space Raaha just created.

### Layer 3 — Memory (closes the loop)

Raaha carries what happened across the day. The message to Reem. The location he moved to. The restoration he chose. At 17:10 it returns — not with a reflection prompt, but with a receipt. *The loop closed. The restoration was chosen. Good work today.*

Memory across the working day is the thread that turns three moments into one experience.

## 5. The moment — Tuesday, 14:20 → 17:10

**The Tuesday.** Late April. Ramadan ended three weeks ago; the rhythm is back to normal hours but the team is still catching up on what didn't get done during shorter days. His calendar has six blocks before lunch.

### 14:20 — the read

Hamad has just come out of a 1:1 that didn't land well. A team member — Reem, two years in, bright, stretched — pushed back on feedback he gave about a draft, and the conversation ended at 14:05 with neither of them settled. He has a team stand-up at 14:30. Ten minutes.

Four meetings in a row. One date and half a sandwich at 11:40. His chest is tight in the specific way that means the last conversation isn't finished.

He opens Raaha.

> Four meetings since 09:30. You last ate at 11:40. Your 14:00 with Reem ended without a close. Your stand-up is in ten minutes.
>
> Want me to push the stand-up to 14:40 and help you close the loop with Reem first?

He taps yes.

### 14:20 → 14:24 — the artefacts (Layer 1: space created)

1. **Raaha moves the stand-up.** Calendar updates live. His team's calendars update.
2. **Raaha drafts the delay note to the team** — warm, brief, Arabic salaam, no over-explanation: *"السلام عليكم — pushing our stand-up to 14:40, ten minutes. Thanks for the flex."* He taps send.
3. **Raaha drafts the close-the-loop to Reem** — three lines. Acknowledges her pushback was fair. Separates the feedback on the draft from his confidence in her. Offers to pick up tomorrow, not now. He changes one word, sends it.

Six minutes back. One cleared loop. Space created.

### 14:24 → 14:28 — the check-in (Layer 2: space inhabited)

Raaha doesn't offer a breathing exercise by default. It asks.

Three swipe prompts, generated live from his day:

> *"Your 1:1 with Reem ran long. How did it land?"* ← still heavy / landed okay →
>
> *"Have you eaten anything today?"* ← I'm fine / I could eat →
>
> *"Your next 1:1 is at 15:00. Want to try something different?"* ← keep as-is / yes →

Each swipe is two seconds of effort. Low disclosure. No form.

Hamad swipes *still heavy* on Reem, *I could eat*, *yes* on something different.

The three swipes route deterministically to one of five outcomes. His pattern lands on **Coffee Shop (change meeting)** — the heaviest intervention on the table, gated by hunger and openness to reshape.

Raaha responds, warm and direct — one proposal, not a menu:

> That one's sitting with you. Change your scenery — shall we take your 1:1 over coffee?

He taps *Send message to attendee*. A short DM drafts, register-tuned: *"Let's meet over coffee — change of pace."* He sends.

Calendar updates. The meeting location changes. A small affirmation lands:

> *Enjoy — take care of yourself.*

One tap (plus send). Two artefacts in the world. The loop closes.

He walks into stand-up at 14:40. Steadier than he walked out of the 1:1 with Reem. At 15:00 he's at a table in the Coffee Shop, not at his desk. They have the conversation they would have had anyway, in a different register.

### 17:10 — the close (Layer 3: memory closes the loop)

His last meeting ended at 17:00. Laptop half-closed. He's about to decide whether to open his inbox one more time before leaving.

Raaha surfaces without him asking.

> Your message to Reem landed. She replied at 16:20 — she said thank you, and that tomorrow works.
>
> You took your 1:1 over coffee this afternoon. Did it help?
>
> Good work today.

No reflection prompt. No five-question end-of-day journal. A receipt, a small acknowledgement of the choice he made, and an affirming close.

He closes the laptop. He's out of the building by 17:20.

## 6. Why AI — not "chatbot," specifically this

**Raaha performs five operations no existing piece of Abu Dhabi government wellbeing infrastructure can perform.**

**AI affordance 1 — real-time synthesis across calendar, comms, and context.**
The first screen is a sentence only Raaha could say to Hamad, because only Raaha has read every calendar invite, meeting title, and message timestamp and noticed the pattern in the ten-minute window before stand-up. Context-reading at the moment of need. ADPHC can't do this. CHOs can't do this. HR portals can't do this.

**AI affordance 2 — multi-artefact orchestration inside a single flow.**
In sixty seconds, Raaha moves a meeting and drafts two messages — one to the team, one to Reem — each in a register appropriate to its audience. The delay note has no over-explanation because a G10 doesn't owe his team one. The message to Reem separates feedback on the draft from confidence in her. **Producing three register-appropriate artefacts across three audiences in one flow is a capability that sits above what any scheduling tool, email assistant, or chatbot offers individually.**

**AI affordance 3 — adaptive restorative prompting.**
The swipe prompts are generated live based on what Raaha read in his day. *"Your 1:1 with Reem ran long. How did it land?"* is a different opener from what Raaha would ask after a morning of calendar-free deep work. **The prompts themselves are an AI affordance, not a fixed script.** This is where the check-in stops being a form and starts being a conversation Raaha only could have today.

**AI affordance 4 — context-carrying across the afternoon.**
The 17:10 beat proves this is a co-pilot, not a chatbot. Raaha opened a loop at 14:20, helped him reshape his afternoon at 14:26, and at 17:10 returned with both — the Reem reply landed, the coffee 1:1 helped, the loop is closed. Three-hour memory applied at the moment the employee is deciding whether to open the inbox one more time. **Nothing in ADPHC, Friends of Happiness, or the CHO program can do this.**

**AI affordance 5 — register-tuned generative voice.**
The delay note opens with *السلام عليكم* because that's how Hamad would write it. The message to Reem is in English because they speak English at work. The 17:10 close lands on *"Good work today"* — affirming, not permission-granting, because paternalistic framing is exactly the register a G10 in Gulf push-through culture has had enough of. **Cultural and professional register, generated live, tuned per audience, is what makes the prototype feel designed by someone who has sat in Abu Dhabi government buildings — not by someone who read about them.**

**The "why AI" test, inverted.** If you removed AI from this flow, what would Raaha become? A calendar app with a rescheduling button, a message template library, a journaling form, and an end-of-day notification. That product exists, and it doesn't win a case study at DGE AI Factory. **AI is not a feature of Raaha — AI is the only reason Raaha is materially different from the tools Hamad has today.**

## 7. How Raaha assists Hamad's wellbeing — rest, delivered

**The counterfactual:** What does Hamad's Tuesday look like without Raaha?

- He reschedules the stand-up in four clicks, writes a defensive delay note that implies he's behind, walks into stand-up still spiked from the Reem conversation.
- He doesn't write Reem until 22:30 from his sofa, with less care, and the message lands worse because the day has intervened.
- He stays at his desk for the full afternoon because no one suggests otherwise.
- He opens his inbox three times between 17:00 and 18:30, checking whether Reem replied.
- He carries Reem home. Tomorrow starts with the same weight.

Multiply across a year, across eleven direct reports, across the one-in-five conversations that don't land cleanly — this is where *"3 in 5 workers can't switch off"* comes from in his specific case.

**What Raaha delivers, mapped to the Arabic meaning of راحة:**

- **Ease** (راحة as ease): the operational friction that would have occupied his afternoon is removed by 14:24.
- **Peace of mind** (راحة as peace of mind): the 17:10 receipt closes the loop Raaha opened, so he doesn't need to carry uncertainty home.
- **Tranquility** (راحة as tranquility): the swipe check-in reshaped a meeting Hamad already had into something restorative — coffee, a change of pace, a conversation in a different register. Grounding through a different shape of work, not a break from it.
- **Rest** (راحة as rest): accumulated over weeks, the capacity this preserves is the difference between a section head who burns out at month 9 and one who leads his team into year seven.

**Against the National Wellbeing Strategy 2031 framing — flourishing, not absence of distress.** Raaha isn't treating Hamad for burnout. It's keeping him in the state where his best work is possible — walking into meetings steady, choosing a moment of grounding in the middle of a hard afternoon, leaving the building at 17:20, being present at his daughter's bedtime. That's flourishing in a civil servant's vocabulary.

## 8. How AI shaped my workflow, and the tools I used when

### Phase 1 — Research synthesis (Day 1, Wednesday)
**Tool: NotebookLM.** Loaded 11 public sources into the corpus: UAE National Wellbeing Strategy 2031, HR Law 08/2025, Workplace Options cross-national analysis, KPMG UAE workplace wellness study, Ellusho burnout analysis, ADPHC Workplace Wellness program, Abu Dhabi Government Digital Strategy 2025–2027, DGE AI transformation briefings, and the Raaha brief itself. Ran four structured queries — wellbeing facts, DGE AI vocabulary, UAE workplace stress statistics, existing employee wellbeing programs.
**Decision enabled:** Hamad as G10 section head, Tuesday afternoon between meetings, specifically because the evidence converges on middle management + manager-tension + back-to-back-meeting stress patterns.

### Phase 2 — Problem framing and persona pressure-test (Day 1–2)
**Tool: Claude chat (extended thinking).** Drafted the persona narrative, then pressure-tested against the NotebookLM corpus in three passes: policy grounding, moment-to-evidence mapping, "why AI uniquely" at moment level.
**Decision enabled:** Single-persona prototype on Hamad. Deck narrates three archetypes for range. Platform argument in the deck; depth in the build.

### Phase 3 — The two-layer architecture decision (Day 2)
**Tool: Claude chat (dialogue).** Early framing was productivity-shaped and didn't deliver the Arabic meaning of Raaha. Through dialogue, the two-layer architecture emerged — orchestration creates space, restoration inhabits it — which resolves the tension between the operational mechanism and the wellbeing purpose.
**Decision enabled:** The swipe check-in and contextual restorative act as Layer 2. This is the hero capability.

### Phase 4 — Visual direction and screen design (Day 2–3, Thursday–Friday)
**Tool: Claude Design (research preview).** Set up the design system in Claude Design first — tokens, typography, component palette — before generating any screens. This is the guardrail that stops Claude Design freelancing on brand. Generated the five screens — the read, the artefacts, the swipe check-in, the restorative act, the 17:10 close. Refined system prompt iteratively until the voice matched Hamad's register.
**Decision enabled:** Screens feel designed by someone who has sat in Abu Dhabi government buildings.

### Phase 5 — Prototype build (Day 3, Friday)
**Tool: Claude Code + Next.js + Tailwind + Vercel.** Handed Claude Design output to Claude Code for the functional build. Wired in the Anthropic API for four live AI calls: the morning read, the delay-note draft, the close-the-loop draft, and the swipe-prompt generation. The swipe-prompt call is the most complex — prompts are generated live based on calendar and comms context.
**Decision enabled:** Live AI in the prototype, not hardcoded scripts. Judges tap, every generation is new.

### Phase 6 — AI behaviour tuning (Day 4, Saturday)
**Tool: Anthropic API with iterated system prompts.** Whole day tuning. Edge cases: empty states, refusals (AI declines to draft a message that'd cross a line), privacy moments (Raaha never references the content of a private conversation, only that one happened), prayer-time awareness, and — critically — the swipe prompts. By Saturday noon, the prompts had to sound like Hamad's Tuesday, not a questionnaire. If they sound like a form, the product collapses back to the Calm comparison.
**Decision enabled:** The prototype feels designed by the environment it's designed for.

### Phase 7 — Process deck (Day 5, Sunday)
**Tool: Claude Design → PPTX export.** Structured as Problem → Persona → Hypothesis → Two-layer architecture → Moment walkthrough → AI workflow receipts → Key decisions → Demo link → What's next.
**Decision enabled:** The deliverable tells the story of how AI built the product, not just what the product does.

## 9. Decisions I made along the way — and why

**1. Cut the new-hire persona (Ahmed) entirely.** Beautiful writing, but failed Pass 3 — his end-of-day reflection moment is something any journaling app does, and the "memory across six months" payoff is undemo-able in a three-minute tap-through. *Depth on the demonstrable beats breadth on the aspirational.*

**2. Cut the frontliner (Mariam) from the prototype; kept her in the deck.** Survives policy grounding and evidence mapping, but building both at polish-grade eats Saturday's tuning day. Platform argument goes in the deck; depth goes in the build.

**3. Held the line at five screens.** Every conversation after Day 2 tried to add a sixth screen — onboarding, settings, role-picker, cumulative record. I held the line. More screens = less polish = worse demo.

**4. Rejected meditation, therapy, gratitude, and habit-tracking features.** These violate the locked design principles and are the exact thing the brief says doesn't fit into an employee's actual day. They exist in the field Raaha is differentiating against.

**5. Added the two-layer architecture after the first framing was challenged.** Early narrative was productivity-shaped and under-developed the wellbeing purpose. The swipe check-in + contextual restorative act emerged from that tension and is now the hero capability. *The fix was architectural, not cosmetic.*

**6. English-first, Arabic switcher as timeboxed feature, killable by Saturday 4pm.** Arabic is important; Arabic done badly is worse than English done well. Risk management decision, not a cultural shortcut.

**7. Live Anthropic API in the prototype, not hardcoded scripts.** Single most consequential technical decision. Judges tap a live prototype and understand in two seconds that this is AI doing real work. *This is where the brief's line — "use it to generate, code, and construct" — earns its keep.*

**8. 17:10 close as the second moment, not a morning check-in.** Morning check-in is a journaling convention. The 17:10 close is the only way to demonstrate memory-across-sessions inside a three-minute demo. *It's not a second feature — it's the first feature finishing its sentence.*

**9. The Tuesday setting inside a post-Ramadan catch-up week.** Specificity, not stereotype. The Ramadan reference isn't paste-on culture — it explains why the team is behind, why the six-meeting morning is plausible. Cultural grounding is structural.

**10. Reem (G8 direct report) is named; the director is not.** Raaha interacts inside Hamad's span of control. It does not touch upward. A wellbeing tool that drafts messages to your director gets uninstalled on day two.

**11. Swipe prompts as the Saturday-morning tuning priority.** If the prompts feel generic, the product collapses. Tuning starts before anything else on Saturday.

- **Mariam's moment (next):** 08:15 shift-opening, calendar read, protected lunch block.

One engine. Many surfacing moments. Across 30,000+ employees. Across their working lives.
