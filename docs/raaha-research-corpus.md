## Section 1 — Employee reality inside Abu Dhabi Government

### ⭐ Abu Dhabi Government HR Law No. 08 of 2025 (effective 1 January 2026)

**Link:** https://www.mediaoffice.abudhabi/en/government-affairs/abu-dhabi-government-issues-new-hr-legislation-modernising-employment-and-embedding-meritocracy-in-public-sector/

**Why this matters to Raaha:** The single most important source in this corpus. Establishes that Abu Dhabi Government is actively competing for talent and has named employee wellbeing as a retention lever. Raaha lands into a policy tailwind, not a vacuum. Use this to defend "why now" in the process deck.

**Key facts for persona work and problem framing:**
- Addressable user base: 25,000+ Abu Dhabi Government employees
- New law introduces: entrepreneurship leave, doubled paternity leave, extended maternity leave
- Flexible work arrangements: compressed schedules, optimised hours, enhanced remote work
- Explicit strategic framing: government must offer "compelling reasons to choose and stay in public service" because talented individuals have private-sector choices
- Targets recruitment in AI, technology, and policy — note the overlap with your DGE audience

---

### ⭐ ADPHC Workplace Wellness & Safety Program

**Link:** https://www.adphc.gov.ae/en/Public-Health-Programs/Workplace-wellness-and-Safety-Program

**Why this matters to Raaha:** The existing institutional wellbeing infrastructure. Raaha's competitive position is *individual and in-the-moment* where ADPHC is *institutional and programmatic*. Use this to position Raaha as complementary, not competitive — that framing lands better with a government audience than "disruption."

**Key facts:**
- Run by Abu Dhabi Public Health Center
- ~38 government entities currently enrolled
- Covers physical activity, nutrition, mental health, ergonomics, stress management, sleep
- Delivery: awareness campaigns, workshops, wellness webinar series, health kiosk screenings
- Operates through HR departments and internal wellness committees

---

### Abu Dhabi School of Government (ADSG) / GovAcademy

**Link:** https://www.dge.gov.ae/en/what-we-do/programs

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Context for employee development culture. ADSG has been subsumed into DGE. Relevant if your persona is actively using leadership/coaching programs and you want to position Raaha as bridging the gap between formal development and day-to-day wellbeing.

**Key facts:**
- Originally established 2018 by Law 15/2018, now part of DGE
- Offers: leadership development, coaching skills, scholarships, public-private exchange
- NYU Abu Dhabi partnership for public policy executive education
- Signals: Abu Dhabi Government employees are supported with structured, formal development — but nothing in-the-moment

## Section 2 — National strategy context (macro-framing for the deck)

### National Strategy for Wellbeing 2031

**Link:** https://u.ae/en/about-the-uae/strategies-initiatives-and-awards/strategies-plans-and-visions/social-affairs/national-strategy-for-wellbeing-2031

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Top-level federal mandate that Raaha laddrs up to. Not a source of product insight — a source of rhetorical grounding for the deck's opening.

**Key facts:**
- Three levels: individuals, society, country
- 14 components, 9 strategic objectives
- Named priorities: mental health, positive thinking, healthy and active lifestyles
- 90 supporting initiatives, 40+ priority areas
- Infrastructure: National Wellbeing Observatory, Academy of Wellbeing, National Wellbeing Council

### National Programme for Happiness & Wellbeing

**Link:** https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/social-affairs/happiness/national-programme-for-happiness-and-wellbeing

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Establishes that "wellbeing" and "happiness" are institutional vocabulary in the UAE, not soft HR language. UAE has had a Minister of State for Happiness & Wellbeing since 2016, and government entities appoint Chief Happiness Officers.

**Key facts:**
- Launched 7 March 2016
- Minister: H.E. Ohood bint Khalfan Al Roumi (Minister of State for Government Development and Future)
- 60 Chief Happiness and Wellbeing Officers trained from federal and local government entities
- Academy of Wellbeing — capacity building for government entities
- Supporting program: "Friends of Happiness" platform

### We the UAE 2031 vision

**Link:** https://wetheuae.ae/en

**Why this matters to Raaha:** The highest-level national vision. "Forward Society" is the pillar Raaha belongs to rhetorically. Use sparingly — one reference in the deck, not more.

**Key facts:**
- Four national priorities: Forward Society, Forward Economy, Forward Diplomacy, Forward Ecosystem
- Launched 22 November 2022 during UAE Government Annual Meetings
- "Forward Society" explicitly covers social empowerment, cultural heritage, education, healthcare

## Section 5 — UAE workplace reality (problem framing stats)

### Daily work-stress — Gallup via Workplace Options

**Link:** https://www.workplaceoptions.com/blog/cross-national-analysis-of-employee-mental-health/

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Core problem-statement stat. Use in the opening of the process deck.

**Key fact:**
- ~40% of UAE employees experience work-related stress on a daily basis
- Contributing factors: lack of manager support, unclear communication, unreasonable time pressure
- 3 in 5 UAE workers say they don't get enough quality time with family because of their job

---

### UAE burnout — KPMG 2023 study

**Link:** https://life.ellusho.com/workplace-burnout-in-the-uae-signs-causes-and-when-to-get-help/

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Secondary validation stat.

**Key fact:**
- 55%+ of UAE professionals reported burnout symptoms (KPMG UAE 2023 workplace wellness study)
- High-burnout sectors: finance, tech, healthcare, real estate

---

### UAE burnout — Bayt.com 2019

**Link:** https://sage-clinics.com/mental-health-in-the-workplace-tips-for-employers/

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Older but widely cited stat. Use if you need a historical comparison point.

**Key fact:**
- 46% of UAE employees experienced burnout in 2019 (Bayt.com)
- 70% attributed burnout to work-related factors
- Top causes: lack of recognition, lack of management support, low job satisfaction

---

### Stigma and culture context

**Link:** https://americancenteruae.com/the-rise-of-workplace-burnout-in-the-uae/

**Why this matters to Raaha:** Informs the AI behavior principles doc (Day 2 deliverable). Mental health stigma is a design constraint — Raaha's tone cannot read as therapy, cannot label feelings clinically, cannot pathologize normal stress.

**Key facts:**
- Stigma around mental health support in UAE professional contexts still meaningful
- Employees hesitant to seek help due to fear of professional repercussions
- Design implication: Raaha needs to be recognisably non-clinical, non-HR-adjacent, private
